Correlations Between Income, Age, Poverty Level Rate & Health

Study 1: Population In Poverty. We notice a positive correlation between poverty and higher health risks. There appears to be a trend in greater poverty rates in southern States, perhaps due to denser populations.

Study 2: Median Age. Overall trends are slightly inconsistent but for specific areas/states trends can be seen clearly. One example is West Viriginia in relation to New England states, higher age medians, better healthcare but West Virginia has more obesity and smokers than New england.

Study 3: Median Household Income. Higher incomes generally lead to healthier lives vs lower income and unhealthy lives. There is a a clear trend with low incomes and an increase of health issues. Northeastern states are much better are much healthier than the southern states.
